Where Every Walk Is an Adventure

!

When a boy named Trey heads out to the park one sunny day, he ﬁnds something he wasn't looking for — a stray dog with a wagging tail and a whole lot of heart. From that moment on, Trey and Clay are inseparable: exploring, discovering, and caring for the animals and people around them, one joyful rhyme at a time.

!
🐾 🐾 🐾
!
The story of A Day in the Park began as a poem written by Ethan Jarmush in the fourth grade — inspired by the true story of his father bringing home a stray dog. That poem won second place at a regional literary contest, and reading it aloud at the awards ceremony sparked a lifelong passion for storytelling. Each book in the series takes its title from the opening line of its own poem — a quiet signature woven through every adventure.

Read It Aloud

!

Bouncy, warm, and made for sharing — a taste of where it all began.

!
!
One day I walked down to the park,
In search of trails I longed to mark,
When suddenly I heard a bark —
And found a friend to play.
!
We circled back to where we met,
Our day of fun not over yet,
A memory I won't forget —
That's when I named him Clay.
!
I woke to licks upon my face,
His tail wagged at twice the pace.
So glad he'd ﬁnally found his place —
My new friend, home to stay!

About the Author

!

Ethan Jarmush is a writer, storyteller, and lifelong animal lover. His journey began in the fourth grade when a poem he wrote — inspired by the true story of his father bringing home a stray dog — won second place at a regional literary contest. Reading it aloud at the awards gala lit a spark that never went out. He has since published a short story and completed a full-length novel. The Adventures of Trey and Clay is the series he has always been meant to share.
